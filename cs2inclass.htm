Why Babson?

I came to after a 11-year stint at Boston University School of Management in 2009. My years at Babson College have been fantastic to say the least. I believe this to be a community of students, faculty, and staff that respect each other and actually support each other's growth. I have had a wonderful ten years and I am sure there will be many more such years to come. I teach in the undergraduate program. In Babson, I taught FME (Foundations of Management and Entrepreneurship) until 2013 and taught SME (Sophomore Management Experience) Information Technology and Management since then. I also teach a couple of Advanced Liberal Arts electives: Web Technologies, Advanced Web Development. For the last three years, I taught a course on Agile Methods. I designed a bootcamp course called "Tech-Savvy Entrepreneurs" to introduce students to coding as an entrepreneurial tool. I am currently teaching a new core course in Operations and Information Management in the graduate program

My Favorite Thing To Do

I love drinking coffee and tea. I love hazelnut flavored coffee and I am so happy that there is now a Starbucks in Babson. I love Dunkin, but, the Starbucks is now so much closer to my office and my class rooms. I am particularly fond of Turkish coffee and tea from India or Sri Lanka. After living in Singapore for two years I have concluded that Green tea has significant health benefits. People that drink it don't often get headaches and cold! So, I have started to drink a lot of Green tea and friends, including Babson students, have helped me stockpile a good supply of Green tea. I also have a collection of spiced tea (masala chai, Tulsi chai and Ginger chai) from India where tea is also called "chai". My favorites are the Darjeeling Tea from India and the Oolong Tea from China. A little known fact - some of the best green tea comes from Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia!!
